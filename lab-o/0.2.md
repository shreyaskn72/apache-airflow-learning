### 0.2 Why Airflow (vs cron, scripts)?


**Limitations of:**
* Cron jobs

Cron jobs are simple and effective for scheduling tasks at specific times, but they lack the ability to manage complex workflows with dependencies. They also do not provide built-in mechanisms for handling retries or monitoring task execution, which can lead to failures going unnoticed. 




* Python scripts

While Python scripts can be used to automate tasks, they often require manual intervention to manage dependencies and scheduling. They may also lack visibility into the execution process, making it difficult to track the status of tasks or identify issues when they arise.

**Benefits of Airflow:**
* Dependency management: Airflow allows you to define dependencies between tasks, ensuring that they are executed in the correct order
* Retry & monitoring: Airflow provides built-in mechanisms for handling retries in case of failures and offers a user-friendly interface for monitoring task execution
* UI visibility: Airflow's web interface provides visibility into the status of workflows and tasks,
* making it easier to identify and troubleshoot issues when they arise.
* Mental model:
* Cron = "run this at time X"
* Airflow = "run this workflow with dependencies, retries, visibility"
* Airflow provides a structured way to manage complex workflows, making it easier to maintain and scale your data pipelines compared to traditional methods like cron jobs or standalone scripts.
